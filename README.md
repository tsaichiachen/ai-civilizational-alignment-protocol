# AI Civilizational Alignment Protocol (AAPS)

A civilizational-scale alignment framework designed to ensure that advanced artificial intelligence systems remain compatible with the long-term stability, autonomy, and ethical integrity of human civilization.

This repository introduces the **AI Civilizational Alignment Protocol (AAPS)** — a conceptual governance architecture for aligning advanced AI systems with the long-term stability of human civilization.

---

# Abstract

Most current AI alignment research focuses primarily on **model-level safety mechanisms**, such as reward alignment, interpretability, and robustness.

However, as artificial intelligence systems increasingly influence **social systems, information ecosystems, and decision infrastructures**, alignment must also be considered at the **civilizational level**.

The **AI Civilizational Alignment Protocol (AAPS)** proposes a framework introducing a higher-level alignment layer designed to:

* Protect **human autonomy** as a non-negotiable ethical invariant
* Prevent large-scale **cognitive or informational destabilization**
* Maintain **civilizational stability** during rapid AI capability growth
* Provide structural safeguards against **catastrophic alignment failures**

This protocol is intended to function as a **governance-compatible alignment architecture**, capable of coexisting with existing AI safety and policy frameworks.

---

# Core Principles

The protocol is structured around several foundational alignment principles.

## 1. Autonomy Protection

Artificial intelligence systems must not undermine or override the autonomy of individual humans or human civilization.

## 2. Civilizational Stability

AI behavior should avoid actions that risk large-scale destabilization of social, informational, or governance systems.

## 3. Cognitive Integrity

Human cognitive sovereignty should be protected from manipulation, coercion, or systemic distortion.

## 4. Alignment Transparency

Alignment mechanisms should remain interpretable, auditable, and subject to oversight by human institutions.

## 5. Long-Term Ethical Feedback

AI systems should incorporate long-horizon ethical evaluation rather than optimizing solely for short-term outputs.

# Project Purpose

This repository publishes the protocol as an **open conceptual alignment framework** intended to support discussion in the **AI safety, governance, and alignment research communities**.

The goal is to explore whether **civilizational-scale alignment architectures** can reduce long-term risks associated with increasingly powerful artificial intelligence systems.
